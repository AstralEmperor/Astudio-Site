MY EXPERIENCE
AS INDIE DEVELOPER

Although my journey as an Indie Game Developer isn't too long, i had my share of experience working alone, with mixture of strugle and satisfaction entwined.

Being an Indie Game Developer gives you an opportunity to work on a project on your own, and make a whole game from scratch exactly how you imagine it to be. Although it can be very stressful sometimes, it is a fun learning experience ,and forces you to constantly think.

Many times you get stuck in place,not knowing what to implement and how..But with modern day internet its not too big of a problem, you can get an idea by just looking at other peoples work,or watching tutorials.


Because i started with a big project right away, i wasnt aware of many quality of life things ,which would of helped me a lot.

For example:

  1. Most Objects should be placed independently instead of with background

  2. Unity has spine animation

  3. Plan everything,and build upon it - not plan as you build

  4. Easier to be overwhelmed, if the project is too big

I suggest for everyone interested to try and do it, it is alright if you are unable to do everything great. The Secret is to Use the Talent you have to the best of potential(music/art/animation/coding). Many games have poor art but amazing gameplay, great environment but low quality entities, or just great music.Any of these is great on its own once given enough attention.
